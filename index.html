About Us

Karonga Debate Club (KADEC) second concept was conceived in 2018 and, after consultation with key stakeholders in Karonga District. The Organisation was then established in 2004 as Youth Organisation that will support national development across Malawi. In 2015 KADEC became a local non-governmental organisation. KADEC follows an empowerment model, which promotes capacity building, socio-economic, political and sustainable development, with the goal to facilitating access to equal opportunities and citizen engagement in social, civic, economic and political life. KADEC later registered its membership to different networks such as Malawi-Scotland Partnership, Youth Act Alliance and Karonga Civil Society Organizations Network.

    • The Karonga Debate Club (KADEC) endeavors to contribute to the enhancement of vulnerable communities' living standards through multifaceted approaches. Our goal is to work in a holistic manner, empowering the most vulnerable populations to actively engage in public life, thus striving towards a comprehensive improvement in their quality of life. Through community mobilization, capacity building, advocacy efforts, and the strengthening of grassroots institutions, KADEC aims to create sustainable change and foster resilience within the communities we serve.

    KADEC basically works mainly for the poorest of the poor in the rural areas of the province. Its target groups include in the poor and disadvantaged rural communities with emphasis on the most vulnerable men, women and children, youth, the elderly, people living with disabilities and orphans in Malawi who are still living in miserable conditions and deprived of very basic necessities of life that. For achieving its goal and objective, KADEC target groups also include health workers, paramedics and other staff related to health issues, teachers, students, environmentalists and stakeholders in the field of health, education and environment.

    • Improve the health conditions of communities through elimination of health hazards in the environment that lead to untimely death among adults and children.
    • Improve learning environment through supporting school learning facilities.
    • Contribute to strengthen democratic governance through advocacy at all levels.
    • Promote and safeguard human rights through civic education and community mobilization.
    • Promote gender equality and the empowerment of women and girls.
    • Create an enabling environment for youth participation in all spheres of life at all levels.
    • Enhance social and economic development and improved quality of life for the poor and disadvantaged rural communities with emphasis on women and children, youth, the elderly, the disabled persons and orphans.
    • Promote and protect child rights through community mobilization.
